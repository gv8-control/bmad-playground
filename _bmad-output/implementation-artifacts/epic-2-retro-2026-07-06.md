## Challenges and Growth Areas

### Recurring Patterns (from deep story analysis)

1. **Sync error codes silently swallowed — 4 of 6 stories (2-2, 2-3, 2-4, 2-5).** `syncArtifactsAction` returns `RATE_LIMITED`, `NOT_FOUND`, `NO_REPO_CONNECTION`, or `UNKNOWN`; the page falls through to empty state with no user feedback. Deferred every time as "spec-mandated" or "DP-5 scope temptation." Root cause: conflating "don't block on sync failure" with "don't inform on sync failure."

2. **`take: 100` silently truncates — 4 of 6 stories (2-2, 2-3, 2-4, 2-5).** No pagination, no "showing N of M" indicator. Deferred as "pre-existing from 2-2" every time; root never fixed.

3. **No concurrency control on page-load sync — 3 of 6 stories (2-1, 2-2, 2-3).** Concurrent sync invocations compute different `scannedPaths` snapshots; one sync's `deleteMany({ notIn })` can wipe rows another sync just upserted. Silent data loss risk.

4. **Story bundling / uncommitted prior work — 2 of 6 stories (2-2, 2-3).** Story 2-1's deliverables uncommitted when 2-2 started; landed in 2-2's diff. Story 2-2's page.tsx never committed; 2-3 found placeholder and rewrote whole page instead of adding `RefreshButton`. Process defect: stories marked `done` before commitment.

5. **Type assertions on DB rows bypass validation — 2 of 6 stories (2-2, 2-3).** `a.type as ArtifactType` with no runtime guard; unknown DB values render empty badges.

6. **`role="listitem"` on `<a>` overrides implicit link role — 2 of 6 stories (2-5, 2-6).** Accessibility trade-off, copied forward.

## Key Insights and Learnings

1. **A deferral that recurs is not a deferral — it's avoidance.** The sync-error-swallowing pattern was deferred 4 times across 4 stories. The deferral rationale ("spec-mandated") was a misreading: the spec said proceed with rendering Postgres data, not "never inform the user sync failed."

2. **Stories marked done before commitment cause bundling and scope creep.** The 2-1→2-2 bundling and 2-2→2-3 placeholder rewrite were caused by marking stories done before they were committed to git. This inflated 2-2's review patch count and caused 2-3 to rewrite a page instead of adding one component.

3. **Deferred retro action items compound.** Epic 1 retro items 3 (auto-analyst) and 6 (trend accumulator) were not done. Had they been done, the sync-error recurrence would have been caught after 2-2 automatically, not manually discovered in this retro by reading all six story files.

4. **Established patterns reduce review patches to zero.** 2-6 had zero patches because 2-5 had established the `<Link>` pattern and 2-6 followed it exactly. Pattern establishment is a leading indicator of story smoothness.

## Significant Discoveries and Epic Update Recommendations

### Significant Change Identified

The autonomous pipeline's "defer-and-move-on" quality gate is accumulating blocking debt rather than resolving it.

**Evidence:**
1. Epic 2 sync-error-swallowing deferred 4 times across 4 stories — never resolved at root.
2. Epic 1 retro action items 3 and 6 (auto-analyst, trend accumulator) not done — the recurrence-detection instrument was never built.
3. Epic 3's remaining stories (3-9, 3-11, 3-12) have blocking dependencies on deferred items from already-shipped Epic 3 work (3-1, 3-2, 3-3, 3-4, 3-6). Current story definitions don't account for resolving that deferred work first.

**Impact on Epic 3:**

The current plan for 3-9, 3-11, 3-12 assumes:
- Idle timeout logic is complete (it's not — "cleared on first message, never restarted")
- Concurrent invocation is guarded (it's not — "concurrent-turn guard missing")
- Sandbox state is recoverable after restart (it's not — in-memory only)

But Epic 2 and early Epic 3 revealed:
- Deferred items compound across stories
- The pipeline has no automated trend detection to surface recurrence
- Stories marked `done` before commitment caused bundling and scope creep

**Epic update required: YES.** Epic 3's remaining story definitions (3-9, 3-11, 3-12) must be updated to explicitly include resolution of their blocking deferred items as prerequisites.
